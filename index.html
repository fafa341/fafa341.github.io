Fabio Fuentes

I'm exploring how mathematics, AI systems, and language can be used to make complex systems more intelligible and humane. My interests sit at the intersection of AI, economics, and human behavior, with a focus on translating messy, subjective knowledge—narratives, expert intuition, and informal decision-making—into structured, computational systems that actually work in the real world.

I learn by building. I've worked on my undergraduate research thesis for my Ingeniería Comercial (economic and administrative sciences) advised by Professor Benjamín Villena-Roldán. I've worked on end-to-end ML projects spanning large-scale text analysis, topic modeling, and applied econometrics, as well as production systems that systematize chaotic, expert-driven workflows in domains like manufacturing.

I care deeply about rigor, interpretability, and measurable impact, and I'm skeptical of AI systems that optimize for fluency without grounding in reality.

I'm motivated by human augmentation rather than replacement, and by tools that help people understand themselves, their work, and their environments better. Outside of tech, I'm an endurance athlete, which shapes how I think about discipline, feedback, and long-term progress.

Research

My work focuses on computational approaches to understanding human systems—bridging LLM's, machine learning, economics, and behavioral science. I'm particularly interested in how we can extract structure and insight from unstructured, qualitative data like narratives and expert knowledge, and apply those insights to real-world decision-making systems.

Signal in the Noise: A State-Dependent Local Projection Analysis of News Narratives and the Chilean Business Cycle
Fabio Fuentes
Working Paper, September 2025
paper / learnings

This research analyzes the relationship between news narratives and the Chilean business cycle using Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) and state-dependent local projection methods. The study explores how media attention and sentiment affect economic outcomes, with a focus on the "attention amplification" effect.
